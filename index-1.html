Bulkhead Calculator

A boxed-out L-section — a vertical face plus a horizontal underside (soffit). Used to drop a perimeter, hide services, or frame a feature.

How a bulkhead is framed: continuous top & bottom rails run the full length on the face, a corner rail runs along the external arris, and a former/bracket is built every 600mm to hold the box square. The face and the underside are then boarded and an angle bead protects the corner.

Which board where?

WallBoard standard ivory

General-purpose board for walls and ceilings in dry areas. The default, cheapest option.

SoundBloc acoustic, blue

Higher-density core for better sound insulation — bedrooms, party walls, cinema rooms, offices.

FireLine fire, pink/red

Glass-fibre reinforced core for fire resistance — protected escape routes, garages, ceilings needing 30–60 min fire rating.

Moisture Resistant green

Water-repellent core for bathrooms, kitchens, utility rooms. Tile onto it or use before wet plastering.

          Site safety

          • Boards are heavy & awkward — two-person lift, use a panel hoist / dead-man prop for ceilings.
          • Dust mask (FFP2+) when cutting or sanding; goggles overhead.
          • Check the soffit for services (cables, pipes, ducts) before drilling fixings.
          • Working at height — proper podium/tower, not a wobbly stack.
          • Isolate the area for silica dust when sanding jointing compound.

          Assumptions & method

          So you can trust the numbers — and check them against the current British Gypsum White Book.

          Frame spacings used

          • MF5 ceiling sections at 450mm centres (single layer 12.5mm board).
          • MF7 primary channels at 1200mm centres.
          • Hangers at 1200mm centres along each primary, first fixing ≤150mm from the wall.
          • Perimeter channel (MF6) around the full room perimeter.
          • Board screws at ~230mm centres → roughly 10 per m² per layer.
          • Channel lengths priced in 3.6m stock lengths; boards default 2.4m × 1.2m.
          These are sensible defaults for a standard single-layer MF ceiling. Always verify against the actual board weight, span, and any fire/acoustic performance spec — heavier or double-boarded ceilings need tighter section and hanger centres. Adjust the spacings under "advanced" on the Room tab and the whole bill of materials recalculates.

          How the counts are worked out

          Lines of frame = ceil(span ÷ centres) + 1. Primary channels run the room length spaced across the width; ceiling sections run the width spaced along the length. Connecting clips = every primary × section crossing. Hangers & soffit cleats = one per hanger position on every primary. Everything is rounded up to whole purchasable units (lengths, boxes, boards, bags) and the waste % is added to board area before rounding.
